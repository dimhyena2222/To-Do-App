### **Potential Solutions**

* **"Magic Scanner" OCR:** A camera-first entry tool that allows Emma to snap photos of whiteboards or syllabi during school. The app extracts dates and titles automatically so she doesn't have to type.
* **Lunch-Break Reminders:** A scheduled nudge during her school "dead time" (lunch) specifically to log assignments while her brain is still in "academic mode."
* **Energy-Availability Survey:** A quick daily check-in (e.g., "How's your battery 1-5?") that reshuffles her schedule based on whether she has volleyball practice or is feeling burnt out.
* **The "Study Quarterback":** A feature that negotiates with her calendar to find the actual time to work, rather than just setting a "due date" alarm she might ignore.
* **Low-Energy Mode:** A filter that hides complex, heavy tasks (like essay writing) and highlights "quick wins" (like flashcards) for when she gets home exhausted from sports.

### **Top Solution**

The single strongest idea is the **"Autonomous Ingestion & Adaptive Scheduler."** By combining AI photo-capture for zero-effort logging with a schedule that shifts tasks based on Emma’s daily energy levels, the app transforms from a passive list into an active digital **Aide**. This wins because it eliminates the "Planning Tax" and the "Decision Fatigue" that lead to procrastination for high-achieving, busy students.
