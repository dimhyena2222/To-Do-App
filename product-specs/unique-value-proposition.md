# Product Positioning Statement: Aide

**Product Name:** Aide

**Target Audience:** High-achieving, "time-poor" high school students

**Core Problem:** Cognitive fatigue and "The Planning Tax"

---

## The Moore Framework Statement

> **For** high-achieving, "time-poor" high schoolers balancing elite academic goals with intensive daily extracurriculars, **who** struggle to bridge the gap between their ambitious goals and their daily physical exhaustion—specifically the "mental tax" of deciding where to start when their energy is lowest;
> **The Aide** is an **AI-powered academic logistics engine** **that** eliminates the "planning tax" by automatically translating overwhelming syllabi and assignments into a prioritized, energy-aware game plan.
> **Unlike Notion**, which requires massive manual setup and often becomes a "procrastination trap" for students, **our product** requires zero-entry workflow through AI ingestion and dynamically shifts tasks based on the user’s real-time energy levels and schedule changes.

| Element | Specification |
| --- | --- |

### Internal Summary for Stakeholders

Aide shifts the student's mental load from **"What should I be doing?"** to **"How do I get this done?"** By targeting the exhausted high-achiever (The "Emma Carter" persona), the brand voice should be supportive, minimalist, and hyper-efficient—acting as a digital "Quarterback" for their academic life.
