# Product Requirements Document

## **Product Overview**

* **Product Name:** Aide
* **One-Sentence Description:** An AI-powered academic logistics engine that eliminates "The Planning Tax" for exhausted high-achievers.
* **Positioning Statement:** For time-poor high schoolers balancing elite academics with intensive athletics, **Aide** is a digital "Study Quarterback" that automatically translates overwhelming syllabi into an energy-aware daily game plan. Unlike Notion, which requires manual setup, Aide uses autonomous ingestion and dynamic scheduling to remove decision fatigue.

---

## **Target User**

* **Persona:** Emma Carter (17, High School Senior)
* **Context:** Aiming for Ivy League admission; maintains a 3.8+ GPA while balancing a 6:00 AM–10:00 PM schedule including varsity volleyball.
* **Key Constraints:** High physical exhaustion post-practice (6:00 PM); limited "deep work" windows; cognitive fatigue leading to "laziness" or avoidance of complex planning.
* **Goals:** Maintain academic excellence without the "cramped" feeling of a mounting, unorganized to-do list.
* **Frustrations:** Forgetting tasks in the Notes app; feeling "unproductive" despite high effort; the mental weight of deciding *what* to do when energy is at its lowest.

---

## **Problem Statement**

Students experience a "Planning Tax"—the mental energy required to organize, prioritize, and start a task—which is highest when their physical energy is lowest.

* **Evidence:** User research (Berci) confirms that procrastination peaks when "sleep hurts focus most" and assignments are left until the minutes before a presentation.
* **Validation:** High-achieving users (Emma) report feeling "cramped" and "anxious" not because they lack discipline, but because the friction of *starting* a heavy task after a 14-hour day leads to avoidance and "last-minute" preparation.

---

## **Proposed Solution**

Aide acts as an active **Decision Engine** rather than a passive list.

* **Core Approach:** **Autonomous Ingestion & Adaptive Scheduling.** Users snap photos of syllabi or whiteboards; Aide’s AI parses the data and negotiates with the user's calendar to find the best "work windows."
* **Key Differentiator:** **Energy-Aware Prioritization.** Instead of a static "To-Do" list, Aide asks for a "Battery Check" and suggests "Quick Wins" (flashcards/small edits) when the user is tired, saving heavy lifts (essay outlines) for high-energy windows.

---

## **Scope**

* **Included in v1:**
* OCR "Magic Scanner" for syllabus/whiteboard ingestion.
* Daily Energy-Availability check-in.
* Automatic task breakdown (e.g., "One slide" instead of "PowerPoint").
* Push notifications timed for peak productivity (Afternoon/Post-practice).


* **Explicitly Excluded:**
* **In-app Note-Taking:** No document editing or essay writing.
* **Social Features:** No peer-to-peer chat or study groups.
* **Gamification:** No "points" or "levels" that add to digital clutter.



---

## **Success Criteria**

* **Logistical Efficiency:** Emma spends **<5 minutes per day** on academic logistics (entry and scheduling).
* **Reliability:** A **100% reduction** in "forgotten" assignments or tasks lost in unstructured notes.
* **Subjective Impact:** A measurable decrease in "starting friction" reported by the user during evening study blocks.

---

### **Vision Summary**

Emma is a disciplined high-achiever who is physically pushed to her limit by a demanding schedule. She faces a constant "Planning Tax" where the mental effort of organizing her work causes her to stall or forget tasks when she is tired. Existing tools like Notion or Apple Notes fail her because they are either too complex (adding more work) or too passive (offering no guidance). **Aide** fills this gap by acting as a "Digital Quarterback" that automates the planning and adapts to her energy levels, ensuring she starts her work without having to think about *how* to organize it.
